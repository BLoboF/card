WOLF

Wolves howl to communicate. if alone, a wolf may howl to attract the attention of his pack. Packs howl together to send territorial messages from one pack to another.

  • Scientific Name: Canis lupus;
  • Average Length: 1.2 - 2 meters;
  • Average Lifespan: 6 - 8 years;
  • Habitat: occours mostly in wilderness and remote areas, particulary in Canada, Alaska and northern United States, Europe and Asia from about 75oN to 12oN.

Wolves are very intelligent animals. They have thick fur to help them survive in a variety of climates. They are found to live in many places throughout the Northern Hemisphere. Wolves live in small groups that are called packs. They can have from 6 to 20 members in them at a time. They have a very large range that they cover from 33 to 6,200 km2. They hunt as a group which makes it possible for them to take down large prey such as moose and elk. This type of kill helps to ensure there is plenty of food to go around for the entire pack.
